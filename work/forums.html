SHAPEWAYS COMMUNITY FORUMS

In June of 2016, a small team at Shapeways completed an epic quest to migrate ~6 years of community forums data to a new software platform. With this migration, we needed a new theme that would play nice on any device, extend our brand guidelines, and scale with our frontend style system over time.

Final implementation of the new forum homepage. See it live. Old one via Wayback.

My role

Define, design, and implement the interfaces needed for Shapeways new forums in less than four weeks.

Project goals

Ensure that the new forum is easy to use on any device, and is consistent with both brand guidelines and frontend patterns.

Improve overall quality of the forum experience by improving typography, layout hierarchy, and visual clarity of the user interfaces.

Shapeways forum view on a medium and small screen for comparison.

My Process

I started by researching the Xenforo frontend system, so that I could learn more about our potential options for the experience. I then worked with Product & Community managers to identify and define the features and experiences our forums needed to support.

From there, I prioritized a list of the core views and components that were needed. With that checklist created, I got to work designing the key experiences for both mobile and desktop, got signoff on them from stakeholders, and then jumped straight into frontend implementation in order to meet the deadline.

By the end of the project, design and development largely happened simultaneously. This was enabled mostly by two things. First, Shapeways has a rubust and reliable CSS style system built on BEM methodologies. Shout out to the incredibly strong frontend team at Shapeways for their expertise in building that system. Secondly, Xenforo's frontent component pattern is easy to work with and scales well. This enabled extensibility and flexibility for pretty much every major view, as well as easy ways to enable our brand colors, fonts, and icons for use in these UI's.

Real interface & content, fake browser.

The results

I met the deadline I set, which was especially important because I was scheduled to start a three week quest/pilgrimage to Scandinavia promptly afterwards (documented here).

Shapeways forums went from being unindexed by search engines and not friendly on mobile, to the #6 result in Google for 3D printing forums, and easy to read and use on a phone to boot.

The new theme is more consistent with brand guidelines, and is much more easy to maintain and update because it extends our style system.

Forum home, search, and thread view on mobile.

Lessons learned

#1 - A solid plan and clear goals will speed up execution. It's easy to make decisions on the fly when you trust in your goals. Does this push my goals forward or hinder the project from reaching them? Is this feature more important to implement than this other one? Refer to the prio list. Rinse, repeat.

#2 - Know where you are on The Scale. Cap Watkins introduced "The Scale" in this fantastic post. He's a smart guy, and that system works incredibly well in my experience. Due to the short timeline of this project, we had to work together to define features we'd support, how to implement them, and how to measure their success pretty quickly. We had to defer to The Scale a few times to quickly resolve arguements, come back from tangents, and stay focused on the prize.
